First Aid + CPR Quick Guide

Offline emergency reference. Keep this as a field guide. Call emergency services as soon as possible when available.

Medical safety note: this app is educational support, not a replacement for certified training or emergency professionals.

Adult CPR (BLS Summary)

  • Check scene safety. Check responsiveness and breathing.
  • Call emergency services / send someone to call.
  • Begin chest compressions: center of chest, 100-120/min, depth 2 in (5 cm).
  • Allow full chest recoil. Minimize pauses.
  • Compression-to-breath ratio: 30:2 if trained and rescue breaths possible.
  • Use AED ASAP and follow prompts.

Child / Infant CPR Quick Differences

  • Child depth about 2 in (5 cm), infant about 1.5 in (4 cm).
  • Use one/two hands for child; two fingers or encircling technique for infant.
  • Same compression rate: 100-120/min.
  • If alone with child/infant and unwitnessed collapse: ~2 minutes CPR before leaving to call (per training protocols).

Conscious Adult/Child Choking

  • Ask “Are you choking?” If they can cough/speak, encourage coughing.
  • If severe obstruction: 5 back blows + abdominal thrusts (per local protocol).
  • Alternate until object clears or person becomes unresponsive.
  • If unresponsive: begin CPR and call emergency services.

Infant Choking

  • 5 back slaps + 5 chest thrusts.
  • No blind finger sweeps.
  • Call emergency services and start CPR if unresponsive.

Severe Bleeding Control

  • Apply direct firm pressure with clean cloth/gauze.
  • If soaking through, add layers and continue pressure.
  • Use tourniquet for life-threatening limb bleeding if trained.
  • Do not remove embedded objects; stabilize around them.
  • Treat for shock and get emergency help fast.

Burn First Aid

  • Cool burn with cool running water for 20 minutes (no ice).
  • Remove tight items before swelling.
  • Cover loosely with clean non-stick dressing.
  • Do not pop blisters or apply butter/oils.
  • Seek urgent care for large/deep/face/hands/genitals/airway burns.

Recovery Position (Breathing but Unconscious)

  • Place nearest arm at right angle, far arm across chest.
  • Bend far knee and roll person onto side toward you.
  • Tilt head back slightly to keep airway open.
  • Monitor breathing continuously until help arrives.
